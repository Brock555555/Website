Worldbuilding in Revenge of the Sith:
This paper analyzes Star Wars Episode III: Revenge of the Sith as both a political warning and a worldbuilding project that reflects on liberty and democracy in modern governance. It argues that George Lucas intentionally drew from historical and political contexts such as the fall of Rome, World War II, and post-9/11 America to illustrate how republics collapse into tyranny when citizens surrender freedoms to corrupt leaders. The downfall of the Jedi and the Galactic Republic is paralleled with the erosion of liberty in the United States, where unchecked power and political complacency threaten democracy. Through visual and narrative analysis, the film is shown to convey a cautionary message: liberty dies not only by force but by public consent. Beyond politics, Revenge of the Sith demonstrates the role of worldbuilding in science fiction as a humanistic teaching tool, shaping discourse, imagination, and cultural understanding. Ultimately, the paper concludes that the film warns audiences to resist complacency, preserve liberty, and engage critically with governance.
